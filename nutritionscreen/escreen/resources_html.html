Eating Habits Survey: Your Resources

General Healthy Eating

EatRight Ontario

If you live in Ontario, call EatRight Ontario. Get free food and nutrition advice from a Registered Dietitian. Call 1-877-510-510-2 or email your nutrition questions. Watch this video to see what happens when you call EatRight Ontario.

If you live outside Ontario, use this Find a Dietitian tool. Find out about what services are available for you.

Visit the EatRight Ontario web site at www.eatrightontario.ca.

  • Find recipes. You can also use the search in the top right to look for recipes with specific ingredients, for example, fish.
  • Watch videos. Topics include: What to expect when you call EatRight Ontario, General healthy eating, Kitchen tips and food handling, reading nutrition information on food labels.
  • Read a Guide to Healthy Eating for Older Adults.  In this guide you will find tips and ideas to make healthy eating easy and enjoyable. You can download to your computer or print a copy for yourself. 
  • Read articles. Review the popular topics such as diabetes and digestive health or enter a search topic in the top right corner. Here are just some of the senior specific articles:
    • Older adults eating well.
    • A matter of taste
    • Managing chewing problems
    • Just for men stay healthy over 50
    • Staying healthy through menopause and beyond
    • Stroke prevention What you need to know
    • Five tips on maintaining your weight as you age

Eating Well with Canadas Food Guide

The Food Guide is your best tool for choosing foods to get the nutrients and energy you need daily. Follow the Food Guide to reduce your risks for many health conditions.

  • View this Food Guide tour.
  • Available in many different languages.
  • Find out how much food you need.
  • Find out how much is a Food Guide Serving.
  • Practical ideas to use the Food Guide every day.
  • Keep track of your daily food choices. Men 51 + or women 51+.
  • Build a Food Guide personalized for you. Print it off and post it on your fridge or another place where you will see it daily.

eaTracker

Track your daily food and activity choices using eaTracker. Get feedback on how your choices compare to Health Canadas recommendations. Register to record your goals and track how well you are doing. Get a nutrient analysis of your favourite recipes.

Colour It Up Program for Ontario women over the age of 50

Start eating more vegetables and fruit using this 6 week community-based program to help you. Find out if there is a program in your community. Fill in this online form or call (416) 971-9800 ext. 3712.
