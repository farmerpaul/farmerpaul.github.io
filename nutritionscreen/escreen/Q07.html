How often do you have milk products?
Examples are fluid milk, cooking with milk, milk puddings, ice cream, cheese, yogurt, and milk alternatives like fortified soy beverages.
  • Think about your typical eating pattern.
  • Think about if you include milk or milk alternatives in your typical breakfast, lunch, dinner and snacks.
  • Milk alternatives include cheese, yogurt, fortified soy beverages and cheese alternatives.
  • Remember to include any amounts of milk mixed into your foods e.g. soup, hot chocolate, puddings/custards, cereal with milk
  • One milk serving = 1 cup of milk/milk substitute, 1 cup cottage cheese, ¾ cup (175 g) of yogurt or kefir, 1 ½ ounces (50 g) of cheese, paneer.
  • Do not count milk in tea or coffee as a serving, but DO count milk with cereal.
  • Only think about your food and beverage choices not supplements [e.g. calcium or Vitamin D pills, calcium fortified juice or other calcium fortified products do not count as milk alternatives.]
